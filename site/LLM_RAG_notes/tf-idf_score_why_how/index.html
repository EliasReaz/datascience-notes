    üìù TF‚ÄëIDF Reference Note

    TF‚ÄëIDF emphasizes word relevancy. In document search, it helps identify which documents are most relevant to a user‚Äôs query by highlighting words that are distinctive to specific documents rather than common across all.

    üîé Why TF‚ÄëIDF is Useful in Search

    • Balances frequency and distinctiveness: Common words like ‚Äúthe‚Äù or ‚Äúand‚Äù are down‚Äëweighted, while rare but meaningful words like ‚Äúclimate‚Äù or ‚Äúinflation‚Äù are up‚Äëweighted.

    • Improves ranking: Documents where query terms have high TF‚ÄëIDF scores are ranked higher because those terms are both frequent and distinctive in those documents.

    • Contextual relevance: A word‚Äôs importance is judged relative to the entire corpus, so search results reflect not just raw frequency but discriminative power.

    1. Core Definitions

    • Term Frequency (TF):
      Frequency of a word within a single document. High if the word appears often in that document.
    • Document Frequency (DF):
      Number of documents in the corpus that contain the word at least once.
    • High DF ‚Üí word appears in many documents.
    • Low DF ‚Üí word appears in few documents.
    • Inverse Document Frequency (IDF):
      Measures rarity across documents:
      [ IDF = \log\left(\frac{N}{DF}\right) ]
      where (N) = total number of documents.
    • Low DF ‚Üí High IDF.
    • High DF ‚Üí Low IDF.
    • TF‚ÄëIDF Score:
      [ TF\text{-}IDF = TF \times IDF ]
      Highlights words that are frequent in one document but rare across the corpus.

    2. Interpretation

    • High TF‚ÄëIDF Score:
    • Word is frequent in a specific document.
    • Word is rare across other documents.
    • Meaning ‚Üí the word is distinctive or signature for that document.
    • Low TF‚ÄëIDF Score:
    • Word appears across many documents.
    • Meaning ‚Üí the word is common background noise (e.g., ‚Äúthe‚Äù, ‚Äúand‚Äù).

    3. Examples

    üìÑ Case B: 1 Book with 5 Pages

    üì∞ Case C: News Articles

    • Word ‚Äúinflation‚Äù appears in 80 articles.
    • DF = 80 ‚Üí IDF = low.
    • Even if TF is high in one article, TF‚ÄëIDF score is lower because the word is common.

    4. Rule of Thumb

    • High score ‚Üí specific to one document.

    • Low score ‚Üí common across all documents.

    • High TF‚ÄëIDF score ‚Üí query word is highly relevant to that document.

    • Low TF‚ÄëIDF score ‚Üí query word is common across many documents (e.g. the, and), less useful for distinguishing relevance.

    • Granularity matters:

    • If you treat each book as a document ‚Üí TF‚ÄëIDF highlights words distinctive to each book.
    • If you treat each page as a document ‚Üí TF‚ÄëIDF highlights words distinctive to each page.
